Engineering Linearity

Preventing "a photo-copy of a photo-copy of a photo-copy" in self-replicating machines.

One of the goals of the reprap community is to get self-replicating machines. This workds pretty alright for the most part. It's more expensive then just buying the parts, and obviously it only works for the plastic components, not the hot-end or motors or control board. But on the whole it's an alright first step.

The big problem is the "photo-copy of a photo-copy of a photo-copy" effect. If you print a 3D printer on a 3D printer, it's slightly less accurate. And so on and so on. This keeps us from true self-replication.

A rack and pinion system printed using a 3D printed rack and pinion system is going to be less accurate then the original. After so many copies, you've run out of lineararity.

Repraps get around this using various "vitamens". Using cold-rolled steal bars instead of 3D printed parts, and getting their linearity from those handfull of "known good" components.

But we have another source of pre-engineard linearity. The clocks on microcontrollers provide a high degree of linearity in the 4th dimension, time.
